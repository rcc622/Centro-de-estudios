Common restrictions to campaign performance
There are many advertiser controls that can be used to optimize campaign performance. You
can adjust elements such as budget, bid strategy, placements, audience and optimization.
When you adjust advertiser controls, you may affect potential reach, spend and cost outcomes.
Setting too many constraints can negatively impact a campaign. For example, when you narrow
a selected audience or set a low bid cap, you limit the options the ad delivery system has to
explore. This limitation prevents the system from finding the lowest cost results. This can also
happen if there are too few ad impressions or not enough conversion events.
These constraints cause a reduction in liquidity, meaning ads won’t appear to people as
frequently. It may also take longer for ad sets to exit the learning phase and optimize their
delivery. Below are the most common restrictions to liquidity.
Placement If you restrict the number of placements for ads, there are fewer opportunities
to optimize.
Audience If your ads reach a very small audience, you restrict the ad delivery system and
limit the optimization opportunities available to it.
Budget If the budget is too small, it may be difficult to achieve 50 conversions per week,
which is the number of conversions recommended to meaningfully optimize a
campaign.
Bid If you set a very low bid or bid cap, you're less likely to see results.
Optimization If you choose an optimization event that's very low in the conversion funnel,
such as sales, you may restrict the number of results and limit optimization
opportunities.

How the ad auction works
Unlike traditional auctions, the ad with the highest monetary bid in the ad auction isn’t
necessarily the winner. Winning ads are determined by the value they offer to both the
business and the people seeing the ads.
An ad’s total value is calculated based on three factors: advertiser bid, estimated action rates
and ad quality. The ad with the highest total value wins each auction.

Bid
This is the amount you bid in the auction and what you are willing to pay for your desired
outcome. A higher bid helps to increase the total value of an ad, which increases its ranking in
the ad auction. There are three types of bid strategies available for the auction buying type:
spend-based, goal-based and manual.
Estimated action rates
This is the likelihood that showing an ad to a person will lead to the desired result. Action rates
are estimated based on previous actions a person has taken and historical ad performance.
When an ad has a high estimated action rate, it’s more likely to achieve results. This can help
increase its total value.
Ad quality
This refers to how relevant an ad may be to the person who sees it. The quality of an ad is
determined by feedback from people viewing or hiding the ad, assessment of clickbait or
engagement bait, and post-click experiences.

Buying types 14

The learning phase
Each time an ad is shown, the ad delivery system learns more about who to show it to and
where. The more an ad is shown, the better the ad delivery system becomes at optimizing
performance.
This is when the learning phase happens. The learning phase is the period when the ad delivery
system is gathering information about how an ad set may deliver and perform. The ad delivery
system explores the best way to deliver an ad set when a new ad or ad set is created or when a
significant edit is made to an existing ad set.
While the delivery system never stops learning, ad sets exit the learning phase as soon as they
can be delivered stably. This usually occurs after about 50 optimization events in the week
after the ad set’s last significant edit.

How the reservation buying type works
When you create a campaign using reservation buying, you agree to pay a set price for the
advertising inventory placement you reserve. The cost of your reservation campaigns is always
charged in CPM, which is cost per thousand impressions. It’s important to know that with
reservation buying, the frequency is an interval and the reach number isn’t 100% guaranteed.
You are only charged for the ads delivered, and you have the flexibility to cancel reservation
campaigns after they start.

Selecting an audience
There are a few important things to remember when selecting an audience for the reservation
buying type.
You can display ads to:
● People across multiple countries at once within a single ad set. You can’t
customize the language in your ad creative to appear in different languages in
other countries, but you can create different ad sets with language-specific
ad creative for each country your ads reach.
● An audience of at least 200,000 people.
You can’t exclude certain audiences.
With the reservation buying type, you can’t exclude custom audiences, people who
have already seen your ads, or people who have taken an action after seeing your
ads. You can exclude audiences with the auction buying type.
Frequency control
Frequency controls enable you to control how often any given person may see your ad. This
can help you balance trade-offs between reaching more people and showing ads more often.
There are two different ways to manage your ad frequency: target frequency and frequency
cap. Both enable you to adjust how many people you’ll be able to reach and how often those
people will see your ads during the campaign.

imagine a customer’s journey from discovery to conversion. With data sources, a business can
gain insights at each stage of the journey, learning what products the customer is interested in.

A budget is the amount of money an advertiser wants to spend on ads. It's also a cost control
tool. It helps control overall spend for a campaign or ad set in the same way that a bid strategy
helps control cost per result. When planning a campaign budget, the approach you take will
vary depending on whether you’re purchasing a campaign through auction or reservation.
Budgets for auction campaigns
A budget strategy can be planned for an entire campaign or per ad set. You can plan a
campaign budget by selecting Advantage+ campaign budget. Alternatively, you can manually
set a budget per ad set.
With Advantage+ campaign budget, your budget is managed using machine learning to get the
best results. Instead of setting individual ad set budgets, you can set one budget for the entire
campaign. This budget has the flexibility to be spent more on ad sets with the best
opportunities and less on underperforming ad sets. The diagram below demonstrates how a

With a daily budget, you plan an average daily spend over the course of a week. Each day, the
ad delivery system will attempt to get roughly your daily budget’s worth of the result you
optimized for. However, there may be days when better opportunities are available. On those
days, the system may spend up to 75% over your daily budget.
With a lifetime budget, you tell the system how much you’re willing to spend over the duration
of a campaign or ad set. This budget is useful if you don’t want to exceed a certain amount of
spend. It works best if you have flexibility with how much you can spend each day. With the
default standard delivery, the system tries to spend the budget evenly over the lifetime of the
campaign or ad set. However, to get the best results, the system may spend more on days
when better opportunities are available, and less on days with fewer opportunities.

the audience it creates.
● Divide very large or diverse source audiences into smaller groups and create lookalikes
from each group.
● Source audience quality is also important. A source audience of best customers could
lead to better results than a source audience of all customers.

The GDPR applies to all companies that process personal data about individuals in the EU,
regardless of where the company is based. Processing personal data refers to anything related
to personal data, including how a company handles and manages data, such as collecting,
storing, using and destroying data.

● Requires consent for personalized ads: The GDPR requires a higher standard of consent

and porting their data.

advertisers on Meta technologies.

requirements, the LGPD establishes a new standard of consent in Brazil and broadens
individuals' rights with respect to accessing and porting their data. The LGPD also establishes
responsibilities for data controllers and data processors.
Here’s how the LGPD affects advertising across Meta technologies:
● Requires consent for personalized ads: Explicit consent is required from individuals for
processing their personal data for advertising.
● Impacts ads audience: Potential signal loss across Meta technologies can occur if people
choose not to share their data.

and Terms of Service.

information held by businesses and the right to opt out of the sale of their personal information.

● Impacts ads audience: Once Limited Data Use is enabled, Meta limits the use of personal

● Prohibits sharing of any health information across Meta technologies.
