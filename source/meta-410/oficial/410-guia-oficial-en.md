Optimization settings
When you select a campaign objective at the campaign level, you’ll also have to make
selections at the ad set level that align with that objective and optimize performance. These
settings include conversion location, conversion event, performance goal and datasets. Not all
campaign objectives require all of these settings. In this section, we’ll take a look at how these
options vary from one campaign objective to the next.
Conversion  This is where you want a desired customer action to occur. While some
location objectives automatically set this to the ad, others enable you to choose from
locations such as website, app, Messenger or Facebook Page.
This is the desired action you want to occur when people see your ad. Not all
Conversion event
conversion locations require a conversion event, and some may require you to
select an ad type or engagement type instead.
Campaign objectives 23

Performance goal This is the outcome you want the ad delivery system to bid on in the auction.
Performance goals vary depending on the objective and conversion location
you choose. For all objectives, you need to choose a performance goal.
Dataset This enables you to connect and manage event data from different sources,
such as a website, app, physical store location or business chats, all in one
place. A dataset won’t always be required. If you do need to add a dataset,
make sure the proper data sources are implemented, such as the Meta Pixel or
the Facebook SDK.
Attribution This helps optimize your campaigns and accurately measure ad performance
models and by customizing your ad attribution. Choose from two attribution models:
settings
● Standard attribution: Optimizes delivery based on selected time
windows and user behaviors, enabling you to credit conversions from
impressions, clicks or video plays.
● Incremental attribution: Uses predictive models to optimize delivery for
incremental conversions, identifying whether an ad caused a conversion.

Aggregated Event Measurement
An important aspect of event tracking is Aggregated Event Measurement. While tracking
events, Meta takes certain measures to preserve people’s privacy. Aggregated Event
Measurement is a protocol that Meta uses to allow for measurement of web and app events
for people using iOS 14 and later devices. This protocol was developed to ensure compliance
with privacy regulations and address signal loss from people who opt out of tracking while also
enabling measurement and optimization of ad performance.
This protocol includes privacy protective measures such as:
Removing personal identifiers that could be used to directly or
indirectly identify an individual.
Adding differential privacy, which involves adding statistical noise to
aggregated data to ensure actions of individuals cannot be identified
or inferred from reports.
Aggregating data across accounts before personalizing and
measuring ads, which means all event measurement and reporting is
done at the group level, not the individual level.

How A/B Testing works
